# Go-To-Market

## Exact target user

**Primary**: Engineering Manager or CTO at a Series A startup (15–60 employees), 3–12 developers on the team. They approved the AI tool budget 6–12 months ago when the team was smaller, never revisited it, and now have 8+ subscriptions across Cursor, GitHub Copilot, Claude, and ChatGPT running simultaneously. They're not overspending on any single tool — they're overspending on the *combination*. Nobody on the team has full visibility into what everyone is actually using.

**Secondary**: Indie hacker or solo founder running a side project. Paying for Claude Pro AND ChatGPT Plus AND Cursor Pro simultaneously. $140+/month on AI tools for a product making $0. They know it's too much but haven't done the math.

## How to get the first 100 users in 30 days with $0

**Week 1 — Hacker News:**
Post "Show HN: I built a free tool that audits your AI tool spend in 60 seconds." Post at 8am ET on a Tuesday. The title is factual and specific — no hype. Target: 50 upvotes → front page → 500 visits → 50 audits completed → 15 emails captured.

**Week 1–2 — Reddit:**
Post the tool in r/SaaS, r/ExperiencedDevs, r/startups. Not as self-promotion — as a comment in existing threads about AI tool costs. "I built something that might help with exactly this — [link]". Find threads by searching "AI tools expensive" or "cursor worth it". One genuine comment in a relevant thread outperforms a dedicated post.

**Week 2 — Direct outreach on X:**
Search X for tweets like "anyone else spending way too much on AI tools" or "cursor copilot which one". Reply directly with the tool link. These are warm leads — they've already expressed the pain. Target 20 replies/day → 5% click through → 10 visits/day → 3 audits/day.

**Week 3 — Indie Hackers:**
Write a product post: "I built the Mint.com for AI tool spend — here's what I learned from the first 50 audits." Include real aggregate data from early users (anonymized). IH readers love data and transparency. This gets shared.

**Week 4 — Newsletter outreach:**
Email 10 developer-focused newsletters (TLDR, Bytes.dev, Cooper Press) with a one-paragraph pitch. Most won't respond. If one with 50k subscribers links to it, that's the whole 100 users in one send.
